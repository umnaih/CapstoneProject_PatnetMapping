FAQ

1 What is a Patent Map?

Patent information collected for a specific purpose of use, and assembled, analyzed and depicted in a visual form of presentation such as a chart, graph or table.

2 What are the features of Patent Maps?

1. A Patent Map is based on patent information
2. A Patent Map has a clear purpose of use.
3. A Patent Map consists of appropriate patent information for the purpose of use.
4. A Patent Map contains organized patent information.
5. A Patent Map presents information visually.

3 What is the use of a Patent Map in business?

1. R&D section.
2. Intellectual property management section.
3. Licensing section.
4. Section in charge of countermeasures against infringements.
5. Corporate strategy section.
6. Human resources section.

4 What are the Methods of Patent-Map-Based Analysis?

1. Analytical Method.
2. Qualitative Analysis.
3. Quantitative Analysis.
4. Index Analysis.

5 What is Qualitative Analysis?

Qualitative Analysis is used to analyze the contents, such as the technical content, of individual patent documents and the results often contain relevant individual patent document numbers.

6 What is Quantitative Analysis?

Quantitative Analysis involves forming a cluster of patents as a parent population for a specific category of patents from the beginning, and then further segmenting or stratifying the patents for quantitatively analyzing them.

7 What is Ranking Map?

A Ranking Map presents the ranking of the number of patents filed by technical element or by right-holder or applicant.

8 What is Portion Rate Map?

A Portion Rate Map is a type of quantitative analysis that displays the frequency of patent citations for different regions of a technology space.

9 What is Share Map?

A Share Map shows who filed an application for a patent relating to a specific technology. It is also used to indicate the distribution of applications filed by technical element which the applications relate to.

10 What is Time Series Map?

A Time Series Map is one of the most basic types of Patent Maps. It is used to collect patent documents filed by a particular right-holder, arrange them by year of filing, and plot the number of patents or patent applications over time.

11 What is Twin Peak Map?

A Twin Peak Map highlights the time lag between the period of a rapid increase and the peak period with respect to technological development of the relevant art. A map built on twin peak analysis is commonly used and compares favorably with a Time Series Map in terms of capacity.

12 What is Bar Chart?

A Bar Chart is a type of visual representation used to display the number of patents in different categories or subcategories.

13 What is Heat Map?

A Heat Map in patent mapping is a visual representation of the density of patents in a particular technology area or industry. It uses color-coding to display the concentration of patents in specific geographic regions or assignee organizations.

14 What is Scatter Chart?

A Scatter Chart is a type of chart used in patent mapping to visualize the relationship between two variables. By using a scatter chart, it is possible to identify clusters of related patents and to visualize the level of citation activity for each cluster. This can provide insights into the technological trends and the competitive landscape of a particular field.

15 What is Word Cloud Map?

A Word Cloud in patent mapping is a visualization technique that displays the most frequently occurring words in a patent database. The words are arranged in a cloud-like shape, with the more frequently occurring words appearing larger in size and the less frequently occurring words appearing smaller.
